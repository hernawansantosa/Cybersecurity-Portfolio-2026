A2 — Discover Security Concepts Used in Public Space
Focus: Public CCTV Security Camera

##Objective
To examine how CCTV cameras in a public space contribute to security and risk management.

##Observation
A surveillance camera was observed installed in a public area such as a shopping centre, train station, or street intersection. 
The camera was mounted high above ground level to monitor a wide surrounding area.

##Security Concepts Identified
1. Deterrence
The visible presence of a camera discourages criminal behaviour by increasing the perceived likelihood of being recorded.

2. Monitoring and Surveillance
The camera enables continuous observation, either through live monitoring or recorded footage for later review.

3. Accountability and Evidence Collection
Recorded footage can be used during investigations to identify individuals or reconstruct incidents.

4. Defense in Depth
CCTV works together with lighting, security personnel, and physical barriers to create layered protection in public environments.

5. Privacy Considerations
While CCTV enhances safety, it also raises privacy concerns, including how long footage is stored, who has access to it, and whether advanced technologies such as facial recognition are used.

##Reflection
Public CCTV systems play an important role in crime prevention and investigation. 
However, their effectiveness depends on proper management, monitoring, and responsible data handling to balance public safety with individual privacy rights.
