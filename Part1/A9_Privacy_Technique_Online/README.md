#A9 — Discover Privacy Technique Used Online  
#Focus: End-to-End Encryption (WhatsApp)

#Objective  
To analyse how end-to-end encryption protects user privacy in online communication platforms.

#Observation  
The screenshot above shows WhatsApp’s notification stating that chats and calls are protected by end-to-end encryption. This means that messages are encrypted on the sender’s device and can only be decrypted by the intended recipient.

#Privacy Technique Identified  
End-to-End Encryption (E2EE)

#Security Concepts  

 1. Confidentiality
 Messages are encrypted so that only the sender and recipient can read them. Even the service provider cannot access the message content.

 2. Encryption in Transit
 Data is protected while being transmitted across networks, preventing interception through man-in-the-middle attacks.

 3. Integrity Protection
 Encrypted communication ensures that messages cannot be altered without detection.

#Analysis  
End-to-end encryption significantly enhances online privacy by ensuring that third parties, including platform providers, cannot read user messages. This protects sensitive information such as personal conversations, shared files, and location data. However, while message content is encrypted, metadata (such as timestamps and contact information) may still be collected.

#Reflection  
End-to-end encryption is a critical privacy technique in modern digital communication. It strengthens user trust and protects against surveillance and unauthorized access. However, users must still remain cautious about phishing and social engineering attacks, as encryption does not prevent human error.
