##A3 — Discover Security Concepts Used in Your Residence
Focus: Student Card Access Control (UniHall)

##Objective
To analyse the security mechanisms used in UniHall dormitory for room and parking access.

##Observation
Access to the dormitory building, individual rooms, and garage parking area requires a student ID card.
The card must be tapped on a card reader to unlock doors or open the parking gate.

##Security Concepts Identified
1. Authentication
The student card verifies the identity of the user before granting access.

2. Authorization
Access is restricted to specific areas based on assigned permissions (e.g., only residents can enter certain floors or parking zones).

3. Access Control
Electronic locking systems regulate entry and prevent unauthorized individuals from entering private spaces.

4. Accountability
Electronic access systems may log entry attempts, allowing tracking of who accessed certain areas and when.

5. Defense in Depth
Card access works together with physical locks, CCTV cameras, and building management policies to create layered security.

##Reflection
The student card system enhances security by restricting access to authorized residents only.
Compared to traditional keys, electronic access control reduces the risk of unauthorized duplication and allows centralized management.
However, if a card is lost or stolen, immediate deactivation is necessary to prevent misuse.
